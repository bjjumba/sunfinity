Problem Statement

Most recent official statistics show that only 26.7% of households have access to electricity, including just 18% in rural areas(Uganda Bureau of Statistics, 2016).


In addition to the limited grid, barriers include high electricity tarrifs and connection charges. Moreover, funding shortages have slowed the the implementation of existing policies, including suspension of the Electricity - Connection Policy in late 2020 (Nabukeera, 2020; Bimbo, McRae, & Steinbuks, 2018)

Our Solution

Works like a simple generator that can be carried around. Has a container in which we can house the inverter, lithium ion batteries and the charge controller.


Its cover consists of two solar panels that are foldable when not in use. (Also has provision to detach and extend panels incase need be). Has AC socket outlets from which house hold appliances can be connected.


Unique Value Proposition

Portable and therefore can be used in different location. Does not require additional electric installation provisions for houses lacking.


Standardization as all components of solar system will be purchased as one.


Target Market

HouseHolds

Households without access to the grid form a major market segment

Shops

Trading centres from a major part of the rural eco-system. Places for charging, electronic repair and other activities

Health Centres

Health centres without access to the grid need portable inexpensivenes solutions to drug storage
